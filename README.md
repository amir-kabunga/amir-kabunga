Amir holds a PhD in psychology 
He has extensive experience in matters relating to mental and maternal health among various professionals, 
His PhD dissertation and earlier research endeavors focused on mental health, medical professionals, psychotherapists, individuals living with HIV, pregnant adolescents, refugees, and expectant mothers. 
He has closely worked with  physicians to improve the mental health of patients. 
He has a special interest in prenatal genetic and pre-cancer gynecological mental health conditions. 
His research interests include stress, coping, depression, maternal mental health post-traumatic stress disorder, emotional intelligence. 
He also took a course and has enough experience in project planning and management. 
He used other research methods and conducted responsible research on HIV/AIDS, among others. 
He has been a Project Implementation Committee (PIC) member of the previous Forgaty International Center (FIC) of the National Institute of Health (NIH) US-funded Health Professional Education Partnership Initiative (HEPI) and Transforming Ugandan Institutions' Training Against HIV (TUITAH) Grant Number R25TW011210, PI Celestino Obua. Consequently, he has conducted HIV-related research and mentored several researchers. 
He has developed close relationships with public and private sector mental health officials and health workers throughout his six-year-old research on HIV and mental health. 
Currently, he is the head of the Department of Psychiatry at the Lira University Faculty of Medicine. 
He possesses the knowledge, leadership experience, training, and drive required to successfully support the implementation of any project.
